He, however, did not for one moment wish to impute any complicity or dishonesty to Mr. Glendinning in connexion with the experiments, for that gentleman appeared (as he had said) merely to have been desirous of arriving at the truth.

Mr. Debenham quite agreed with Mr. Bridge as to Mr. Glendinning’s bonâ fides in the matter. He would propose, if possible, a committee be formed to be present at some similar séance.
