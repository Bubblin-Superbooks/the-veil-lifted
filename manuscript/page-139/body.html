These pages—like everything else from Dr. Wallace’s pen—are worthy of careful study, the following sentences particularly so:—

“It may be as well to clear away a popular misconception. Mr. G. H. Lewes advised the Dialectical Committee to distinguish carefully between ‘facts and inferences from facts.’ This is especially necessary in the case of what are called spirit photographs. The figures which occur in these, when not produced by any human agency, may be of ‘spiritual’ origin, without being figures ‘of spirits.’ There is much evidence to show that they are, in some cases, forms produced by invisible intelligences, but distinct from them.
